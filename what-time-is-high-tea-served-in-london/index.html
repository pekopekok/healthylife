In London, high tea is a beloved tradition enjoyed by locals and tourists alike. The meal is typically served in the mid-afternoon and includes a variety of sweet and savory treats. If you're planning a trip to London and want to experience high tea, it's important to know what time it's served so you can plan accordingly. In this article, we'll discuss what time high tea is served in London, as well as some tips for finding the best high tea experiences in the city.

How to Experience High Tea in London

Before we get into what time high tea is served, let's first discuss how to experience it in London. There are many places throughout the city that offer high tea, from fancy hotels to charming tea rooms. Some of the top places to experience high tea in London include:

  • The Ritz London
  • The Savoy
  • Claridge's
  • The Wolseley
  • Browns Hotel
  • Fortnum & Mason

These are just a few of the many options available, so be sure to do your research and find the best high tea experience for your tastes and budget.

What Time is High Tea Served in London?

Now, let's get to the question at hand: what time is high tea served in London? Generally, high tea is served between 3:00pm and 5:00pm. However, it's important to note that some places may offer high tea outside of these hours, so it's always best to check with the specific location you plan to visit.

If you're looking for a traditional high tea experience, it's best to aim for the mid-afternoon time frame. This is when most places will be serving their high tea menus, and it's also when you'll be able to enjoy the full experience with other guests.

Step-by-Step Guide to Enjoying High Tea in London

Now that you know what time high tea is served in London, here's a step-by-step guide to enjoying the experience:

  1. Choose a location: Research and choose a high tea location that fits your preferences and budget.
  2. Make a reservation: Many high tea locations require reservations, so be sure to make one in advance.
  3. Dress appropriately: High tea is a formal affair, so dress appropriately for the occasion.
  4. Arrive on time: Be sure to arrive on time for your reservation to ensure the best experience.
  5. Enjoy the food: Indulge in the sweet and savory treats that are typically served during high tea.
  6. Sip your tea: High tea wouldn't be complete without a cup of tea, so be sure to savor every sip.
  7. Relax and enjoy the experience: High tea is meant to be a relaxing and enjoyable experience, so take your time and soak it all in.

Tips for Finding the Best High Tea in London

With so many high tea options available in London, it can be overwhelming to choose the best one for you. Here are some tips to help you find the perfect high tea experience:

  • Read reviews: Check out online reviews to see what other guests have to say about their experiences.
  • Consider your budget: High tea can be expensive, so consider your budget when choosing a location.
  • Try something unique: Some locations offer unique twists on traditional high tea, so don't be afraid to try something new.
  • Choose a location with a view: Many high tea locations offer stunning views of the city, so choose one with a view for an extra special experience.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about high tea in London:

Q: Is high tea the same as afternoon tea?

A: While the terms are often used interchangeably, high tea typically includes more substantial food items and is served later in the day than afternoon tea.

Q: What should I wear to high tea?

A: High tea is a formal affair, so dress appropriately in smart casual or formal attire.

Q: How much does high tea typically cost in London?

A: High tea can range in price from around £30 to over £100 per person, depending on the location and menu.

Pros and Cons of High Tea in London

Here are some pros and cons to consider when deciding whether to experience high tea in London:

Pros:
  • High tea is a beloved tradition in London and offers a unique cultural experience.
  • The food and tea served during high tea are typically of high quality and delicious.
  • High tea locations often offer stunning views of the city.
Cons:
  • High tea can be expensive, especially at some of the more luxurious locations.
  • The formal dress code may not be to everyone's liking.
  • Some people may find the food items served during high tea to be too rich or heavy.

Overall, high tea is a must-try experience for anyone visiting London. With so many options available, there's sure to be a high tea experience that fits your tastes and budget. Just be sure to plan ahead and arrive on time to ensure the best experience possible.
