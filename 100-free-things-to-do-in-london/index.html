If you're looking to explore London without breaking the bank, you're in luck! There are plenty of free activities and attractions to keep you entertained. From world-renowned museums to stunning parks, this guide will provide you with 100 free things to do in London.

Museums

London is home to some of the world's most renowned museums, and the best part is that many of them are free! Here are some of our favorites:

The British Museum

The British Museum is one of the world's oldest and largest museums, and it's free to enter! You can explore over two million years of human history, from ancient Egypt to modern-day Europe.

National Gallery

The National Gallery houses over 2,300 paintings, including famous works by Van Gogh, da Vinci, and Monet. Admission is free, but some special exhibitions may require a fee.

Tate Modern

The Tate Modern is a must-see for art lovers. The museum is home to contemporary and modern art, including works by Picasso, Warhol, and Hockney. Admission is free, but some exhibitions may have a fee.

Parks and Gardens

London is known for its stunning parks and gardens, and many of them are free to visit. Here are a few of our favorites:

Hyde Park

Hyde Park is one of London's largest parks, and it's free to enter. You can take a stroll through the beautiful gardens, rent a bike, or even go for a swim in the Serpentine.

Kensington Gardens

Kensington Gardens is located next to Hyde Park and is home to Kensington Palace. The gardens are free to enter, and you can enjoy a peaceful walk or a picnic on the grass.

Regent's Park

Regent's Park is home to the London Zoo and the stunning Queen Mary's Gardens. The park is free to enter, and you can enjoy a picnic, rent a boat, or visit the Open Air Theatre.

Architecture and Landmarks

London is full of iconic landmarks and stunning architecture, and many of them are free to visit. Here are a few of our favorites:

The Tower of London

The Tower of London is one of London's most famous landmarks. While admission to the tower may require a fee, you can see the tower from the outside for free.

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the official residence of the Queen and is open to the public during the summer months. While tours may require a fee, you can see the palace from the outside for free.

The Houses of Parliament

The Houses of Parliament are home to the iconic Big Ben and are free to visit. You can take a tour of the building, watch a debate in the House of Commons, or simply admire the stunning architecture.

Markets and Shopping

London is a shopper's paradise, and there are plenty of markets and shopping areas to explore. Here are a few of our favorites:

Borough Market

Borough Market is one of London's oldest and largest food markets. You can sample delicious food, buy fresh produce, and browse the artisanal crafts for free.

Camden Market

Camden Market is a vibrant market full of unique stalls selling everything from vintage clothing to handmade jewelry. You can browse the stalls, listen to live music, and soak up the atmosphere for free.

Oxford Street

Oxford Street is one of London's most famous shopping streets, and while shopping may require a fee, you can window shop and people-watch for free.

Entertainment and Events

London is home to a vibrant entertainment scene, and there are plenty of free events to enjoy. Here are a few of our favorites:

The Changing of the Guard

The Changing of the Guard is a famous ceremony that takes place outside Buckingham Palace. You can watch the guards march and perform their duties for free.

Notting Hill Carnival

The Notting Hill Carnival is a two-day celebration of Caribbean culture that takes place in August. You can enjoy live music, street food, and colorful costumes for free.

Sunday UpMarket

The Sunday UpMarket is a weekly market in Brick Lane that features food stalls, vintage clothing, and handmade crafts. You can browse the stalls and enjoy the lively atmosphere for free.

Food and Drink

London is known for its diverse food scene, and there are plenty of free food and drink events to enjoy. Here are a few of our favorites:

Chocolate Festival

The Chocolate Festival is a free event that takes place in December and features chocolate stalls, tastings, and demonstrations.

London Coffee Festival

The London Coffee Festival is a free event that takes place in April and features coffee tastings, workshops, and competitions.

Real Food Festival

The Real Food Festival is a free event that takes place in May and features food stalls, cooking demonstrations, and talks on sustainable food practices.

History and Heritage

London is full of history and heritage, and there are plenty of free events and attractions to explore. Here are a few of our favorites:

The Monument

The Monument is a 202-foot-tall column that was built to commemorate the Great Fire of London. You can climb the stairs to the top for free and enjoy stunning views of the city.

The Museum of London

The Museum of London is a free museum that explores the history of London from prehistoric times to the present day. You can see artifacts from the Great Fire of London, learn about Roman London, and see modern-day exhibits.

The Natural History Museum

The Natural History Museum is a free museum that is home to a vast collection of dinosaur skeletons, fossils, and taxidermy animals. You can learn about the natural world and even see a life-size model of a blue whale.

Art and Galleries

London is home to a thriving art scene, and there are plenty of free galleries and exhibitions to explore. Here are a few of our favorites:

The Serpentine Gallery

The Serpentine Gallery is a free gallery that showcases contemporary art and architecture. You can see works by famous artists like Jeff Koons and Marina Abramovic.

The Saatchi Gallery

The Saatchi Gallery is a free gallery that showcases contemporary art from around the world. You can see works by emerging artists and established names alike.

The National Portrait Gallery

The National Portrait Gallery is a free gallery that is home to portraits of famous people throughout British history. You can see portraits of everyone from Queen Elizabeth I to David Beckham.

Nature and Wildlife

London may be a bustling city, but there are still plenty of opportunities to connect with nature and wildlife. Here are a few of our favorites:

The Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew

The Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew is a free botanical garden that is home to over 50,000 plants from around the world. You can explore the gardens and even see the world's largest Victorian glasshouse.

The Wetland Centre

The Wetland Centre is a free nature reserve that is home to a variety of birds, insects, and other wildlife. You can take a walk along the wetland trails and even go birdwatching.

The Horniman Museum and Gardens

The Horniman Museum and Gardens is a free museum and gardens that is home to over 350,000 objects, including musical instruments, natural history specimens, and anthropological artifacts. You can also explore the gardens and see the stunning views of London.
