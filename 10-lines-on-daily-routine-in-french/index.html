10 Lines On Daily Routine In French

Written by Jordan May 15, 2023 · 2 min read

.

Description

If you're learning French and want to talk about your daily routine, it's important to know some basic phrases. In this article, we'll go through 10 lines that you can use to describe your daily routine in French. From waking up in the morning to going to bed at night, we've got you covered.

How to

To use these phrases, simply substitute your own activities for the ones listed. For example, instead of "Je me lève à 7 heures," you could say "Je me lève à 6 heures." Practice these phrases regularly to improve your French speaking skills.

Step by Step

1. Je me lève à 7 heures - I wake up at 7 o'clock 2. Je prends une douche - I take a shower 3. Je m'habille - I get dressed 4. Je prends le petit déjeuner - I have breakfast 5. Je vais au travail - I go to work 6. Je déjeune à midi - I have lunch at noon 7. Je travaille jusqu'à 17 heures - I work until 5 o'clock 8. Je rentre chez moi - I go home 9. Je dîne à 19 heures - I have dinner at 7 o'clock 10. Je me couche à 22 heures - I go to bed at 10 o'clock

Tips

To make these phrases more natural, try to use contractions where appropriate. For example, instead of "Je vais au travail," you could say "J'vais au taf." This is more colloquial and will help you sound more like a native speaker.

Solution

If you're struggling to remember these phrases, try writing them down in a notebook and reviewing them regularly. You could also make flashcards with the phrases on one side and their English translations on the other.

FAQ

Q: Are these phrases suitable for beginners? A: Yes, these phrases are fairly basic and should be easy for beginners to learn. Q: Can I use these phrases in any French-speaking country? A: Yes, these phrases are generally understood throughout the French-speaking world.

Pros and Cons

Pros: - These phrases are easy to learn and remember - They cover a range of daily activities - Using them regularly will improve your French speaking skills Cons: - They may not be suitable for more advanced learners - They don't cover every possible daily activity
