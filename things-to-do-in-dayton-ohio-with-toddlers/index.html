Things To Do In Dayton Ohio With Toddlers

If you're looking for fun things to do in Dayton, Ohio with your little ones, then you're in the right place. Dayton is a beautiful city with plenty of kid-friendly activities that will keep your toddlers entertained and engaged. In this guide, we'll explore some of the best things to do in Dayton, Ohio with toddlers.

Table of Contents

  • Visit the Parks
  • Explore the Museums
  • Meet the Animals at the Zoo
  • Visit the Playgrounds
  • Splash Around at the Waterparks
  • Attend Local Events

Visit the Parks

Dayton is home to several beautiful parks that are perfect for toddlers to explore. Some of the most popular parks include:

  • Wegerzyn Gardens MetroPark - This park has beautiful gardens, nature trails, and a play area.
  • RiverScape MetroPark - This park has a large fountain and splash pad that toddlers will love.
  • Cox Arboretum and Gardens MetroPark - This park has gardens, trails, and a play area with a treehouse.

These parks are a great way for toddlers to get some fresh air, exercise, and explore nature. Make sure to bring sunscreen, water, and snacks to keep your little ones happy and hydrated.

Explore the Museums

Dayton is also home to several museums that are perfect for toddlers. Some of the most popular museums include:

  • Boonshoft Museum of Discovery - This museum has interactive exhibits, a planetarium, and a zoo.
  • National Museum of the United States Air Force - This museum has aircraft exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Dayton Art Institute - This museum has art exhibits and interactive activities for kids.

These museums are a great way for toddlers to learn and explore new things. Make sure to check their websites for special events and exhibits.

Meet the Animals at the Zoo

Dayton is home to a fantastic zoo that toddlers will love. The Zoo is a great place to meet and learn about animals from all over the world. Some of the most popular exhibits include:

  • Africa - This exhibit has lions, giraffes, and zebras.
  • Asia Quest - This exhibit has tigers, camels, and red pandas.
  • North America - This exhibit has bears, wolves, and bald eagles.

Make sure to bring sunscreen, water, and snacks to keep your little ones happy and hydrated. There are also several playgrounds and picnic areas throughout the zoo.

Visit the Playgrounds

Dayton has several fantastic playgrounds that are perfect for toddlers. Some of the most popular playgrounds include:

  • Deeds Park - This playground has a large play area, swings, and slides.
  • Wesleyan MetroPark - This playground has a play area, swings, and a splash pad.
  • Washington Township Recreation Center - This playground has a play area, swings, and a climbing wall.

These playgrounds are a great way for toddlers to burn off some energy and make new friends. Make sure to bring sunscreen, water, and snacks.

Splash Around at the Waterparks

If you're looking for a fun way to cool off on a hot day, then Dayton has several fantastic waterparks. Some of the most popular waterparks include:

  • The Beach Waterpark - This waterpark has water slides, a lazy river, and a wave pool.
  • Great Wolf Lodge - This indoor waterpark has water slides, a lazy river, and a kiddie pool.
  • Splash Moraine - This outdoor waterpark has water slides, a lazy river, and a kiddie pool.

These waterparks are a great way for toddlers to cool off and have fun. Make sure to bring sunscreen, water, and snacks.

Attend Local Events

Dayton is home to several family-friendly events throughout the year. Some of the most popular events include:

  • Dayton Air Show - This event has aircraft displays and performances.
  • Dayton Celtic Festival - This event has music, food, and activities for kids.
  • Dayton Holiday Festival - This event has a tree lighting, parade, and activities for kids.

These events are a great way for toddlers to experience the local culture and have fun. Make sure to check their websites for dates and times.

Conclusion

There are plenty of things to do in Dayton, Ohio with toddlers. From parks and museums to zoos and waterparks, Dayton has something for everyone. Make sure to plan ahead, bring sunscreen, water, and snacks, and have fun!
