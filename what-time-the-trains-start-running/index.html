If you're planning a trip by train, it's essential to know what time the trains start running so that you can plan your journey accordingly. In this article, we'll be discussing the times that trains typically start running, so you can be well-informed and prepared for your next train journey.

Train Times

The precise times that trains start running will vary depending on the train company and the specific route you're travelling on. However, as a general rule of thumb, trains tend to start running from around 5 am in the morning, with the last trains usually departing around midnight.

It's worth noting that this is just an estimate, and the exact times will differ from company to company and route to route. To get a more accurate picture of the times that trains start running on your route, it's best to check the train company's website or speak to a customer service representative.

Early Morning Trains

If you're an early riser, you'll be pleased to know that many train companies run early morning services. These trains tend to start running from around 5 am, making them ideal for commuters or travellers who want to get an early start on their day.

However, it's worth noting that early morning trains can be busier than other services, so it's best to book your tickets in advance to guarantee a seat.

Weekend Trains

Weekend train services tend to start running slightly later than weekday services. Many train companies will start running services from around 6 am on a Saturday and Sunday, with the last trains departing around midnight.

If you're travelling on a weekend, it's worth checking the train company's website to see if there are any changes to the regular service. Some train companies may run different timetables on weekends or bank holidays, so it's always best to check before you travel.

Public Holiday Trains

Public holiday train times will vary depending on the train company and route. Some train companies may run a reduced service on public holidays, while others may run a completely different timetable.

If you're travelling on a public holiday, it's best to check the train company's website or speak to a customer service representative to get an accurate picture of the train times on your route.

Tips for Planning Your Journey

Here are some tips to help you plan your train journey:

  • Check the train company's website for accurate train times
  • Book your tickets in advance to guarantee a seat
  • Consider travelling during off-peak times to save money
  • Check for any service disruptions or engineering works before you travel
