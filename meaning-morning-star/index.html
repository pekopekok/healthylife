What is Morning Star?

The term morning star is often used to describe the planet Venus. Venus is the second planet from the sun and is the brightest object in the sky after the sun and the moon. It is often called the morning star because it is visible in the eastern sky just before sunrise. However, Venus is not a star, it is a planet.

Origin of the Term Morning Star

The term morning star has its roots in ancient mythology. In Greek mythology, the morning star was associated with the goddess Eos, who represented the dawn. In Roman mythology, the morning star was associated with the goddess Aurora. In both cases, the morning star was seen as a symbol of new beginnings and hope.

How is Morning Star Used Today?

Today, the term morning star is often used as a metaphor for hope, new beginnings, and positive change. It is also used in religious contexts to refer to Jesus Christ, who is often referred to as the morning star in the Bible. In the book of Revelation, Jesus says, “I am the Root and the Offspring of David, and the bright Morning Star.”
