Build Big Shoulders Without Weights The Ultimate Guide

Written by Alvine Mar 14, 2023 · 4 min read

.

If you're looking to build big, strong shoulders, you don't necessarily need to hit the gym and lift weights. There are plenty of effective exercises you can do at home or outside using just your bodyweight. Not only will these exercises help you build muscle and strength, but they'll also improve your overall fitness and posture. In this article, we'll explore how to build big shoulders without weights, including exercises, tips, and FAQs.

Description

Building big, strong shoulders can help you achieve a more balanced and aesthetically pleasing physique. Strong shoulders also play an important role in many sports and daily activities, such as carrying heavy objects or pushing yourself up from the ground. While weightlifting is often the go-to method for building shoulder muscles, it's not the only option. In fact, bodyweight exercises can be just as effective, if not more so, for building big shoulders.

How To Build Big Shoulders Without Weights

To build big shoulders without weights, you'll need to focus on exercises that target the three main muscles of the shoulder: the anterior deltoid, medial deltoid, and posterior deltoid. These muscles are responsible for raising your arms to the front, side, and rear, respectively. Some effective bodyweight exercises for building shoulder muscles include: - Push-ups: Push-ups work not only your chest but also your shoulders and triceps. To target your shoulders more specifically, try diamond push-ups or pike push-ups. - Pike shoulder press: This exercise involves getting into a pike position (hips up, legs straight) and then lowering your head towards the ground, keeping your elbows tucked in. - Handstand push-ups: Handstand push-ups are a challenging exercise that require a lot of strength and balance. To perform them, kick up into a handstand position against a wall and then lower your head towards the ground, keeping your elbows tucked in. - Plank shoulder taps: This exercise involves getting into a plank position and then tapping your opposite shoulder with your hand, alternating sides. - Superman push-ups: This is an advanced exercise that involves performing a push-up and then arching your back and lifting your arms and legs off the ground.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to performing some of the exercises mentioned above: Pike shoulder press:
  1. Get into a pike position with your hips up and your legs straight.
  2. Place your hands on the ground shoulder-width apart, fingers pointing forward.
  3. Lower your head towards the ground, keeping your elbows tucked in.
  4. Push back up to the starting position.
  5. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Handstand push-ups:
  1. Kick up into a handstand position against a wall.
  2. Lower your head towards the ground, keeping your elbows tucked in.
  3. Push back up to the starting position.
  4. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Plank shoulder taps:
  1. Get into a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Lift one hand off the ground and tap your opposite shoulder.
  3. Return your hand to the ground and repeat on the other side.
  4. Alternate sides for the desired number of reps.

Tips for Success

- Start with easier variations of the exercises and work your way up to more advanced versions. - Focus on proper form and technique to ensure you're targeting the right muscles and avoiding injury. - Incorporate these exercises into a well-rounded fitness routine that includes cardio, strength training, and flexibility work. - Be patient and consistent with your workouts, and give your muscles time to rest and recover between sessions.

Solution to Common Problems

- If you're having trouble performing the exercises, try modifying them to make them easier. For example, you can do push-ups on your knees or against a wall to start. - If you're not feeling a burn in your shoulders, make sure you're using proper technique and engaging the right muscles. - If you're experiencing pain or discomfort during the exercises, stop and consult a healthcare professional.

FAQs

Q: Can I build big shoulders without weights?
A: Yes, you can! Bodyweight exercises can be just as effective, if not more so, for building shoulder muscles. Q: How often should I do shoulder exercises?
A: It depends on your fitness goals and current fitness level. Aim to do shoulder exercises 1-3 times per week, with at least one rest day in between sessions. Q: Do I need any equipment to do these exercises?
A: No, you don't. All of the exercises mentioned in this article can be done using just your bodyweight.

Pros and Cons

Pros: - Bodyweight exercises are free and can be done anywhere. - These exercises can be just as effective as weightlifting for building shoulder muscles. - Bodyweight exercises improve overall fitness and posture. Cons: - Bodyweight exercises may not be challenging enough for advanced lifters. - It can be harder to track progress with bodyweight exercises compared to weightlifting. - Without proper technique and form, bodyweight exercises can lead to injury.
