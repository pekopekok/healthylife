Introduction

Hindi is one of the most spoken languages in the world. It is the official language of India and is spoken by more than 500 million people worldwide. If you want to learn Hindi and communicate with native speakers, you need to understand the daily routine words used in India. These words are used in everyday conversations, so it's important to be familiar with them. In this article, we will cover the most common daily routine words used in Hindi.

Morning Routine

The morning is the start of a new day, and it's important to have a good routine to kick start your day. Here are some common words used in Hindi to describe your morning routine:

  • Uthna: To wake up
  • Nahaana: To take a bath
  • Dhona: To wash clothes
  • Nashta: Breakfast
  • Jana: To go

So, your morning routine in Hindi would be something like this:

Mein subah uthna, nahaana aur nashta karna pasand karta hu. Phir mein kaam ke liye jata hu.

(I like to wake up, take a bath, and have breakfast in the morning. Then I go to work.)

Work Routine

After your morning routine, it's time to go to work. Whether you work in an office or from home, here are some common words used to describe your work routine in Hindi:

  • Kaam: Work
  • Kaarya: Task
  • Chai peena: To drink tea
  • Khaana: Lunch
  • Ghar aana: To come back home

So, your work routine in Hindi would be something like this:

Mein office mein kaam karta hu. Mere paas bahut saare kaarya hote hai. Dophar mein mein chai peeta hu aur khaana khaata hu. Shaam ko mein office se ghar aata hu.

(I work in an office. I have a lot of tasks to do. In the afternoon, I drink tea and have lunch. In the evening, I come back home from the office.)

Afternoon Routine

After a long work day, it's important to take a break and relax. Here are some common words used in Hindi to describe your afternoon routine:

  • Aaraam karna: To take rest
  • Padhna: To read
  • Likna: To write
  • Chai peena: To drink tea
  • Sone jaana: To go to sleep

So, your afternoon routine in Hindi would be something like this:

Shaam ko mein aaraam karta hu. Mein pustak padhta hu aur kuch likhta hu. Phir mein chai peeta hu aur sone jaata hu.

(In the evening, I take rest. I read books and write something. Then I drink tea and go to sleep.)

Evening Routine

After taking a nap, it's time to get ready for the evening. Here are some common words used in Hindi to describe your evening routine:

  • Saaf-safai: Cleaning
  • Bistar sajana: To make the bed
  • Kapde badalna: To change clothes
  • Bazaar jana: To go shopping
  • Khelna: To play

So, your evening routine in Hindi would be something like this:

Shaam ko mein saaf-safai karta hu aur bistar sajata hu. Mein kapde badalta hu aur bazaar jata hu. Kabhi-kabhi mein dost ke saath khelta hu.

(In the evening, I clean my room and make the bed. I change clothes and go shopping. Sometimes I play with my friends.)

Night Routine

The night is the end of the day and it's important to have a good routine to have a good sleep. Here are some common words used in Hindi to describe your night routine:

  • Raat ka khana: Dinner
  • Bistar par sona: To sleep on the bed
  • Machliyon ko khana dena: To feed the fish
  • Raat ko sote samay: While sleeping at night
  • Jagna: To wake up

So, your night routine in Hindi would be something like this:

Shaam ko mein raat ka khana khaata hu. Phir mein bistar par sota hu. Mein machliyon ko khana deta hu. Raat ko sote samay mein sapne dekhta hu. Subah mein jaldi jaga hu.

(In the night, I have dinner. Then I sleep on the bed. I feed the fish. While sleeping at night, I dream. I wake up early in the morning.)

Conclusion

Learning Hindi and understanding the daily routine words used in India can be challenging, but with practice and patience, you can improve your skills. In this article, we have covered the most common daily routine words used in Hindi. We hope this guide has helped you and you can use these words in your everyday conversations. Keep learning and practicing!
