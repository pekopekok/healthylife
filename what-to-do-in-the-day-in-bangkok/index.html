What To Do In The Day In Bangkok A Guide To Relaxing Activities

If you're planning a trip to Bangkok, you won't be disappointed with the myriad of activities available to you during the day. From exploring ancient temples to indulging in delicious street food, Bangkok has something for everyone. In this guide, we'll take a look at some of the best things to do in the day in Bangkok.

1. Visit the Grand Palace

The Grand Palace is a must-see attraction in Bangkok. This stunning complex of buildings was once the residence of the Kings of Siam and is now used for official events and ceremonies. The Grand Palace is open daily from 8:30 am to 3:30 pm and costs 500 baht per person. Be sure to dress appropriately, as there is a strict dress code in place.

2. Wander Through Wat Phra Kaew

Located within the Grand Palace complex, Wat Phra Kaew is a stunning temple that houses the famous Emerald Buddha. The temple is open daily from 8:30 am to 3:30 pm and costs 500 baht per person. Be sure to take your time exploring the intricate architecture and beautiful grounds.

3. Explore Chatuchak Weekend Market

If you're in Bangkok on a weekend, be sure to check out the Chatuchak Weekend Market. This massive market is home to over 8,000 stalls selling everything from clothing to home goods. The market is open on Saturdays and Sundays from 9 am to 6 pm.

4. Indulge in Street Food

Bangkok is famous for its street food, and no trip to the city is complete without indulging in some delicious local eats. From pad thai to mango sticky rice, there's something for everyone. Be sure to try some of the street food stalls near the popular tourist areas like Khao San Road and Sukhumvit.

5. Go on a Canal Tour

One of the best ways to see Bangkok is by taking a canal tour. These tours take you through the city's network of canals, providing a unique perspective on the city's history and culture. Be sure to book a tour with a reputable company and bring plenty of sunscreen and water.

6. Relax at Lumpini Park

If you're in need of a break from the hustle and bustle of the city, head over to Lumpini Park. This massive park is home to lush greenery, a lake, and plenty of walking paths. Be sure to bring a picnic and relax in the shade.

7. Visit the Jim Thompson House

The Jim Thompson House is a must-see attraction for anyone interested in Thai culture and history. This beautiful house was once the home of American entrepreneur Jim Thompson, who is credited with reviving Thailand's silk industry. The house is open daily from 9 am to 6 pm and costs 200 baht per person.

8. Take a Cooking Class

If you're a foodie, consider taking a cooking class in Bangkok. These classes teach you how to cook traditional Thai dishes using fresh ingredients and traditional techniques. Be sure to book a class with a reputable company and bring your appetite.

9. Visit the Bangkok Art and Culture Centre

The Bangkok Art and Culture Centre is a hub for contemporary art in the city. The centre hosts exhibitions and events throughout the year, showcasing the work of local and international artists. The centre is open daily from 10 am to 9 pm and is free to enter.

10. Watch a Muay Thai Fight

Muay Thai is Thailand's national sport, and watching a fight is a must-do activity for anyone visiting Bangkok. There are several stadiums in the city that host fights throughout the week, with the most popular being Lumpinee and Rajadamnern. Be sure to book your tickets in advance and arrive early to get a good seat.

Conclusion

As you can see, there's no shortage of things to do in the day in Bangkok. Whether you're interested in history, culture, or food, Bangkok has something for everyone. Be sure to plan your itinerary ahead of time, wear comfortable shoes, and bring plenty of sunscreen and water. Happy travels!
