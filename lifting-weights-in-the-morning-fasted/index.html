Lifting Weights In The Morning Fasted Benefits And How To Do It

Lifting weights in the morning fasted has become a popular trend in the fitness industry. It involves performing weightlifting exercises on an empty stomach, typically first thing in the morning, before consuming any food. This technique has a range of benefits, including improved fat burning, increased metabolism, and higher energy levels throughout the day. In this article, we’ll explore how to lift weights in the morning fasted, the benefits, and some helpful tips to get you started.

How to Lift Weights in the Morning Fasted

The process of lifting weights in the morning fasted is relatively simple. However, it requires some preparation to ensure that you get the maximum benefits. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare the Night Before

Before going to bed, make sure to have everything ready for your morning workout. This includes setting out your workout clothes, filling up your water bottle, and preparing any supplements you need to take.

Step 2: Wake Up Early

Set your alarm clock to wake up at least an hour before your workout. This will give your body enough time to wake up and get ready for exercise.

Step 3: Hydrate

Drink a glass of water as soon as you wake up. This will help to hydrate your body and prepare it for exercise.

Step 4: Warm-Up

Start with a light warm-up to prepare your body for exercise. This can include stretching, walking, or light cardio exercises.

Step 5: Start Your Workout

Begin your weightlifting workout, focusing on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups. This can include exercises like squats, deadlifts, and bench presses.

Step 6: Cool Down

Finish your workout with a cool-down period that includes stretching and light cardio exercises. This will help to prevent muscle soreness and injury.

The Benefits of Lifting Weights in the Morning Fasted

There are several benefits to lifting weights in the morning fasted, including:

Improved Fat Burning

When you lift weights on an empty stomach, your body is forced to burn fat for energy. This can help to increase fat burning during your workout and throughout the day.

Increased Metabolism

Lifting weights in the morning fasted can help to increase your metabolism. This means that your body will burn more calories throughout the day, even when you’re not exercising.

Higher Energy Levels

Many people report feeling more energized throughout the day after lifting weights in the morning fasted. This is because exercise releases endorphins, which can help to improve your mood and energy levels.

Tips for Lifting Weights in the Morning Fasted

To get the most out of your morning fasted workout, here are some helpful tips to keep in mind:

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water before and after your workout to stay hydrated.

Take It Slow

If you’re new to lifting weights in the morning fasted, start slow and gradually increase your intensity over time.

Listen to Your Body

If you feel lightheaded or dizzy during your workout, stop immediately and rest.

Get Enough Sleep

Make sure to get enough sleep the night before your early morning workout to ensure that you have enough energy to power through.

Solution to Common Questions

Q: What should I eat before my morning fasted workout?

A: It’s best to avoid eating anything before your morning fasted workout. However, if you need some energy, you can consume a pre-workout supplement or drink black coffee.

Q: How often should I lift weights in the morning fasted?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and schedule. However, many people find that lifting weights in the morning fasted 2-3 times per week is effective.

Q: Will lifting weights in the morning fasted cause muscle loss?

A: No, lifting weights in the morning fasted will not cause muscle loss. However, it’s important to make sure that you’re consuming enough protein throughout the day to support muscle growth and recovery.

Pros and Cons of Lifting Weights in the Morning Fasted

Like any fitness technique, lifting weights in the morning fasted has its pros and cons. Here are some to consider:

Pros:

  • Improved fat burning
  • Increased metabolism
  • Higher energy levels

Cons:

  • Possible lightheadedness or dizziness
  • Requires waking up early
  • May not be suitable for everyone

In conclusion, lifting weights in the morning fasted can be an effective way to improve fat burning, increase metabolism, and boost energy levels. By following the steps outlined in this article and incorporating some helpful tips, you can make the most out of your morning fasted workouts.
