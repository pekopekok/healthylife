New Orleans is a city like no other, with its vibrant culture, delicious food, and rich history. If you only have one day to explore this city, it can be overwhelming to decide what to do. That's why we've put together this guide to help you make the most of your time in New Orleans. From exploring the French Quarter to trying out some of the city's famous cuisine, there's something for everyone in NOLA.

Start Your Day with Beignets and Coffee

There's no better way to start your day in New Orleans than with a plate of warm, fluffy beignets and a cup of coffee. Head over to Cafe Du Monde in the French Quarter and enjoy this classic New Orleans breakfast. Be prepared for a line, though -- Cafe Du Monde is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.


Explore the French Quarter

The French Quarter is the heart of New Orleans, and there's so much to see and do here. Take a stroll down Bourbon Street and enjoy the colorful buildings and lively atmosphere. Stop by Jackson Square and admire the stunning St. Louis Cathedral. Don't forget to take a photo with a street performer or two!


Visit the Historic St. Louis Cemetery

The St. Louis Cemetery is a fascinating glimpse into New Orleans' history. This cemetery is the final resting place of many famous New Orleanians, including voodoo queen Marie Laveau. Take a guided tour to learn more about the cemetery's unique above-ground tombs and the stories of those buried here.


Grab Lunch at the Famous Central Grocery

No trip to New Orleans is complete without trying a classic Muffuletta sandwich from Central Grocery. This Italian deli has been a local institution since 1906, and their Muffuletta sandwich is a must-try. Made with Italian cured meats and olive salad on a sesame seed bun, it's a delicious and hearty lunch option.


Take a Stroll Through City Park

If you need a break from the hustle and bustle of the French Quarter, head over to City Park. This sprawling park is home to beautiful gardens, walking paths, and even a small amusement park. Take a leisurely stroll through the park and enjoy the tranquility of nature.


Try a Hurricane at Pat O'Brien's

Pat O'Brien's is a New Orleans institution, and their signature drink, the Hurricane, is a must-try. This fruity cocktail is made with rum, passion fruit syrup, and lime juice, and it's served in a signature hurricane glass. Enjoy a drink (or two) at Pat O'Brien's famous courtyard bar.


Indulge in Some Po'boys for Dinner

Po'boys are another classic New Orleans dish, and there are plenty of places to try them in the city. Head over to Parkway Bakery & Tavern for a delicious and authentic po'boy experience. Choose from classic options like fried shrimp or roast beef, or try something new like their hot sausage po'boy.


Listen to Live Music on Frenchmen Street

New Orleans is known for its vibrant music scene, and there's no better place to experience it than on Frenchmen Street. This area is home to some of the city's best live music venues, from jazz clubs to blues bars. Spend your evening hopping from venue to venue and enjoying the sounds of New Orleans.


End Your Night with a Ghost Tour

New Orleans is a city with a rich and spooky history, and there are plenty of ghost tours to choose from. Take a walking tour through the French Quarter and learn about the city's haunted past. Whether you believe in ghosts or not, it's a fun and spooky way to end your day in New Orleans.


Final Thoughts

New Orleans is a city that's full of life, culture, and history. While one day isn't nearly enough time to see everything this city has to offer, we hope this guide has given you some ideas for how to make the most of your time here. Whether you're sipping a Hurricane at Pat O'Brien's or strolling through the French Quarter, there's something for everyone in New Orleans.
