Push Day Workout Routine for Beginners | Beginner's Guide 2023

If you're new to working out, you may be wondering what a push day workout routine is and how to do it. A push day workout routine is a type of workout that focuses on pushing exercises, such as chest presses, shoulder presses, and tricep extensions. This type of workout is great for building upper body strength and muscle.

How to Do a Push Day Workout Routine

To do a push day workout routine, you will need access to a gym or home gym equipment. You will also need to warm up before starting your workout to prevent injury. This can include a few minutes of light cardio or dynamic stretching.

Next, you will perform a series of exercises that focus on pushing movements, such as chest presses, shoulder presses, and tricep extensions. You can do these exercises using free weights, weight machines, or resistance bands.

It's important to use proper form when doing these exercises to prevent injury and get the most out of your workout. Start with lighter weights and gradually increase the weight as you get stronger.

Step-by-Step Guide to Push Day Workout Routine

Here's a step-by-step guide to a push day workout routine for beginners:

  1. Warm up for 5-10 minutes with light cardio or dynamic stretching.
  2. Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps of chest presses using dumbbells or a weight machine.
  3. Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps of shoulder presses using dumbbells or a weight machine.
  4. Perform 3 sets of 8-12 reps of tricep extensions using dumbbells or a weight machine.
  5. Finish with 5-10 minutes of light cardio or static stretching.

Tips for Beginners

Here are some tips for beginners doing a push day workout routine:

  • Start with lighter weights and gradually increase the weight as you get stronger.
  • Use proper form to prevent injury and get the most out of your workout.
  • Take breaks as needed and listen to your body.
  • Stretch before and after your workout to prevent injury and improve flexibility.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking water before, during, and after your workout.

Solution to Common Beginner's Problems

Here are some common beginner's problems and solutions:

  • Problem: Feeling intimidated at the gym.
    Solution: Start with a beginner's workout routine and don't be afraid to ask for help or hire a personal trainer.
  • Problem: Feeling sore after your workout.
    Solution: Stretch before and after your workout, and take breaks as needed.
  • Problem: Not seeing results.
    Solution: Make sure you're using proper form, gradually increasing the weight, and staying consistent with your workouts.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about push day workout routine for beginners:

  • Q: How often should I do a push day workout routine?
    A: You can do a push day workout routine 1-2 times per week, depending on your fitness level and goals.
  • Q: Can I do a push day workout routine at home?
    A: Yes, you can do a push day workout routine at home using dumbbells, resistance bands, or a weight machine.
  • Q: How long should a push day workout routine be?
    A: A push day workout routine should be 30-60 minutes long, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Pros and Cons of Push Day Workout Routine

Here are some pros and cons of push day workout routine:

Pros:

  • Great for building upper body strength and muscle.
  • Can be done using free weights, weight machines, or resistance bands.
  • Can be customized to fit your fitness level and goals.

Cons:

  • Doesn't focus on other muscle groups, such as your legs or back.
  • Can lead to muscle imbalances if not balanced with other types of workouts.
  • May require access to a gym or home gym equipment.

Overall, a push day workout routine can be a great addition to your fitness routine if you're looking to build upper body strength and muscle. Just make sure to use proper form, start with lighter weights, and stay consistent with your workouts.
