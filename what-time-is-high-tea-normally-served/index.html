History of High Tea

High tea originated in the 19th century in England, when the working class would come home from a long day of work and have a hearty meal of meat, vegetables, bread, and tea. This meal was known as "high tea" because it was eaten at a high table, as opposed to the lower tables used for afternoon tea. Over time, high tea became more of a social event, with the upper classes adopting the tradition and adding in more luxurious foods like cakes and pastries.

What Time is High Tea Normally Served?

High tea is typically served in the late afternoon or early evening, anywhere from 3pm to 6pm. It's a great way to take a break from the day, relax, and enjoy some delicious treats. If you're planning to go to a high tea, it's always a good idea to make a reservation in advance to ensure you get a table.

What to Expect from a High Tea Experience

When you go to a high tea, you can expect to be served a variety of teas, along with a selection of sandwiches, scones, cakes, and pastries. The sandwiches are typically small finger sandwiches with fillings like cucumber and cream cheese, smoked salmon, and egg salad. Scones are served with clotted cream and jam, and cakes and pastries can include anything from macarons to chocolate truffles to miniature cheesecakes. It's a great way to indulge in some sweet treats and catch up with friends or family.

FAQs

What's the difference between afternoon tea and high tea?

Afternoon tea is a lighter meal consisting of tea, sandwiches, and cakes, and is typically served mid-afternoon. High tea is a more substantial meal consisting of tea, sandwiches, scones, cakes, and pastries, and is typically served later in the afternoon or early evening.

Do I need to make a reservation for high tea?

Yes, it's always a good idea to make a reservation in advance to ensure you get a table.
