Morning Run Vs Evening Which One Is Better

Written by Bobby Mar 26, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're trying to decide whether to go for a morning run or an evening run, you're not alone. Many runners struggle with this decision, and there are pros and cons to both options. In this article, we'll take a closer look at the benefits and drawbacks of morning runs vs evening runs, and help you determine which one is right for you.

Description

Before we get started, it's important to note that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the morning run vs evening run question. The best time of day to exercise depends on your individual preferences, schedule, and lifestyle. However, there are some general guidelines and factors to consider when making this decision.

How to Decide

The first step in deciding whether to run in the morning or evening is to consider your schedule. If you have a busy day ahead of you, you may want to get your workout done first thing in the morning. On the other hand, if you have a more relaxed schedule, you may prefer to run in the evening when you can take your time and enjoy the scenery.

Another factor to consider is your personal preferences. Some people are naturally early birds and enjoy waking up early to exercise, while others are night owls who prefer to work out in the evening. There's no right or wrong answer here – it's simply a matter of what works best for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're still not sure whether to run in the morning or evening, here's a step-by-step guide to help you make your decision:

  1. Consider your schedule and lifestyle. Are you a morning person or a night owl? Do you have a lot of commitments during the day?
  2. Think about your goals. Are you training for a race or just trying to stay in shape?
  3. Test both options. Try running in the morning for a week, and then switch to running in the evening for a week. See which one you prefer.
  4. Listen to your body. Pay attention to how you feel during and after your runs. Do you have more energy in the morning or evening?

Tips for Morning and Evening Runs

Whether you decide to run in the morning or evening, there are some tips that can help you get the most out of your workouts:

  • Stay hydrated. Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your runs.
  • Wear reflective clothing if you're running in the dark.
  • Stretch before and after your runs to prevent injury.
  • Start slowly and gradually increase your pace and distance.
  • Enjoy the scenery and listen to music or a podcast to make your runs more enjoyable.

The Solution

So, which is better – morning runs or evening runs? The truth is, there's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. It all depends on your individual preferences and schedule. However, there are some general guidelines to keep in mind:

  • Morning runs can help you start your day off on the right foot and give you an energy boost.
  • Evening runs can help you de-stress after a long day and improve your sleep quality.

Ultimately, the best time of day to exercise is the time that works best for you. So, try both morning and evening runs, and see which one you prefer. The most important thing is to make exercise a regular part of your routine.

FAQs

Q: Should I eat before a morning run?

A: It's a good idea to eat a light snack before a morning run to give you the energy you need. Try a banana, a piece of toast with peanut butter, or a small bowl of oatmeal.

Q: What should I wear for an evening run?

A: If you're running in the dark, it's important to wear reflective clothing to make sure you're visible to drivers. You may also want to wear a headlamp or carry a flashlight.

Pros and Cons

Pros of Morning Runs:

  • You'll start your day off with a sense of accomplishment.
  • You'll have more energy and focus throughout the day.
  • The weather is often cooler in the morning.

Cons of Morning Runs:

  • You may have to wake up earlier than you'd like.
  • You may feel groggy or sluggish at first.
  • You may have to deal with darkness or cold weather in the winter months.

Pros of Evening Runs:

  • You can de-stress after a long day at work.
  • You can enjoy the scenery and fresh air.
  • You may sleep better after an evening run.

Cons of Evening Runs:

  • You may be too tired or busy to exercise in the evening.
  • You may have to deal with darkness or cold weather in the winter months.
  • You may have to deal with distractions or safety concerns if you're running in a busy area.

Ultimately, the decision to run in the morning or evening is up to you. Consider your schedule, personal preferences, and goals, and try both options to see what works best for you. With a little trial and error, you'll find the perfect time of day to get your sweat on.
