How Many Calories Do I Need A Day If I Workout

If you're wondering how many calories you need a day if you workout, you've come to the right place. The number of calories you need depends on various factors such as age, gender, weight, height, and physical activity level. In this article, we'll discuss how to calculate your daily calorie intake, how to adjust it for your workouts, and other tips to help you achieve your fitness goals.

How to Calculate Your Daily Calorie Intake

The first step to determine how many calories you need a day if you workout is to calculate your basal metabolic rate (BMR), which is the number of calories your body burns at rest. You can use an online BMR calculator to get an estimate or use this formula:

BMR = 10 x weight (in kg) + 6.25 x height (in cm) - 5 x age (in years) + 5

Once you know your BMR, you can calculate your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE) by multiplying your BMR by an activity factor that reflects your physical activity level:

Activity LevelActivity Factor
Sedentary (little or no exercise)1.2
Lightly Active (light exercise or sports 1-3 days a week)1.375
Moderately Active (moderate exercise or sports 3-5 days a week)1.55
Very Active (hard exercise or sports 6-7 days a week)1.725
Super Active (very hard exercise or sports, physical job or training twice a day)1.9

Your TDEE is the number of calories you need to maintain your current weight. If you want to lose weight, you need to create a calorie deficit by eating fewer calories or burning more through exercise. If you want to gain weight, you need to eat more calories than your TDEE.

How to Adjust Your Calorie Intake for Workouts

If you're working out regularly, you need to adjust your calorie intake to fuel your workouts and support muscle growth and recovery. The amount of calories you need depends on the type and intensity of your workouts. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Light exercise: Add 150-200 calories to your TDEE
  • Moderate exercise: Add 250-300 calories to your TDEE
  • Intense exercise: Add 400-500 calories to your TDEE

It's important to note that you should not eat back all the calories you burn during exercise. Your calorie intake should be based on your TDEE plus the extra calories needed for your workouts. Eating too much can lead to weight gain, while eating too little can compromise your performance and recovery.

Tips for Achieving Your Fitness Goals

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve your fitness goals:

  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein, complex carbs, and healthy fats
  • Stay hydrated by drinking enough water and electrolyte-rich fluids
  • Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and alcohol
  • Get enough rest and recovery to allow your body to repair and grow
  • Track your calories and workouts to monitor your progress and adjust as needed

FAQ

How many calories should I eat to lose weight?

To lose weight, you need to create a calorie deficit by eating fewer calories than your TDEE. A safe and sustainable rate of weight loss is 1-2 pounds per week, which requires a daily deficit of 500-1000 calories. For example, if your TDEE is 2000 calories, you need to eat 1500-1700 calories a day to lose 1-2 pounds per week.

How many calories should I eat to gain muscle?

To gain muscle, you need to eat more calories than your TDEE and engage in resistance training to stimulate muscle growth. A calorie surplus of 250-500 calories per day is usually sufficient for muscle gain, but the exact amount depends on your body type, training level, and genetics. Eating enough protein (1.6-2.2 grams per kilogram of body weight) is also crucial for muscle growth.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Calculating your daily calorie intake can help you achieve your fitness goals
  • Adjusting your calorie intake for workouts can improve performance and recovery
  • Eating a balanced diet and tracking your calories can promote overall health and wellbeing

Cons:

  • Calculating and tracking your calories can be time-consuming and tedious
  • Strict calorie counting can lead to an unhealthy obsession with food and body image
  • Everyone's calorie needs are different, and online calculators may not be accurate for everyone

Overall, calculating your daily calorie intake and adjusting it for your workouts can be a useful tool for achieving your fitness goals. However, it's important to remember that calorie counting is not the only factor that determines health and wellbeing. Listening to your body, eating a variety of nutritious foods, and engaging in enjoyable physical activity are also key to a healthy and happy life.
