Welcome to Fort Leonard Wood! If you're here, it's probably because you're about to start your basic training. Congratulations! Basic training is a challenging but rewarding experience that will help you grow physically, mentally, and emotionally. In this article, we will guide you through the basic training schedule Fort Leonard Wood and provide you with tips on how to make the most out of your training.

Table of Contents

  2. What is the basic training schedule at Fort Leonard Wood?

  4. FAQs about basic training at Fort Leonard Wood

When does basic training start?

The start date of your basic training will depend on your assigned unit and the dates set by the Army. You will receive a notification from your recruiter or unit commander about your start date. It's important to prepare yourself physically, mentally, and emotionally for the challenges that lie ahead.

What is the basic training schedule at Fort Leonard Wood?

The basic training schedule at Fort Leonard Wood is designed to help you develop the skills and discipline necessary to become a soldier. Your day will start early, usually around 5:00 am, and end around 9:00 pm. Here's a typical daily schedule:

Morning

  • 5:00 am - Wake up
  • 5:30 am - Physical training (PT)
  • 6:30 am - Breakfast
  • 7:30 am - Personal hygiene
  • 8:00 am - Formation and training

Afternoon

  • 12:00 pm - Lunch
  • 1:00 pm - Training
  • 5:00 pm - Dinner
  • 6:00 pm - Personal time
  • 9:00 pm - Lights out

The training will cover a range of topics, including marksmanship, first aid, land navigation, and drill and ceremony. You will also undergo physical and mental tests to assess your fitness and readiness for service.

Tips for a successful basic training

Basic training can be physically and mentally challenging, but with the right mindset and preparation, you can succeed. Here are some tips to help you make the most out of your training:

  • Stay physically fit before and during basic training
  • Follow instructions and be disciplined
  • Stay mentally tough and focused
  • Help your battle buddies and work as a team
  • Stay positive and motivated

FAQs about basic training at Fort Leonard Wood

Q: What should I bring to basic training?

A: You should only bring the essentials, such as clothes, toiletries, and personal items. Your unit will provide you with everything else you need.

Q: Can I communicate with my family during basic training?

A: Yes, you can communicate with your family through letters, phone calls, and sometimes email, depending on your unit's policies.

Q: What happens after basic training?

A: After basic training, you will attend Advanced Individual Training (AIT) to learn the skills necessary for your assigned job in the Army.

Q: What if I struggle during basic training?

A: It's normal to struggle during basic training, but don't give up. Talk to your drill sergeants or chaplain for support and guidance. Remember, you're not alone, and your battle buddies are there to help you.

Conclusion

Basic training at Fort Leonard Wood is a challenging but rewarding experience that will help you develop the skills and discipline necessary to become a soldier. By following the training schedule, staying physically and mentally fit, and staying positive and motivated, you can succeed in your training and become an asset to the Army. Good luck, and we'll see you on the other side!
