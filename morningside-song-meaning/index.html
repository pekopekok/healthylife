What is Morningside?

Morningside is a song by New Zealand singer and songwriter Amelia Murray, who goes by the stage name Fazerdaze. The track is the opening song from her debut album "Morningside," which was released in 2017. The album received critical acclaim and was praised for its dreamy, lo-fi sound and introspective lyrics.

The Meaning Behind the Song

At its core, Morningside is a song about nostalgia and the passing of time. Murray reflects on her past and wonders what the future holds, expressing a sense of uncertainty and fear of the unknown. The lyrics are deeply personal and introspective, painting a vivid picture of a young woman grappling with her place in the world.

The song opens with the lines "I'm feeling low, feeling high, feeling everything in between," setting the tone for the rest of the track. Murray goes on to sing about the passing of time and how the world around her is changing. She reflects on her childhood and the memories she holds dear, singing "I remember when we were young, staying up late, sneaking out, having fun."

As the song progresses, Murray becomes increasingly introspective, singing "I don't know where I'm going, but I'll get there in the end." She acknowledges the fear and uncertainty that comes with growing up and trying to find one's place in the world, but ultimately expresses a sense of hope and determination.

Why is Morningside So Popular?

Morningside is a popular song due to its dreamy, introspective sound and relatable lyrics. Murray's voice is soft and ethereal, perfectly complementing the dreamy guitar riffs and lo-fi beats. The song's lyrics are deeply personal and relatable, speaking to anyone who has ever felt lost or unsure about their place in the world.
