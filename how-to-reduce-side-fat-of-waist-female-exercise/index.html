How to Reduce Side Fat of Waist Female Exercise

Reducing side fat of waist can be challenging, but with the right exercises and a proper diet, it's possible. The best exercises for reducing side fat of waist are:

  • Oblique Crunches
  • Side Plank Dips
  • Woodchoppers
  • Bicycle Crunches
  • Leg Raises

These exercises target the obliques, the muscles on the side of your waist. By doing these exercises regularly, you can tone and strengthen these muscles, which will help reduce side fat of waist.

Oblique Crunches

Oblique crunches are one of the most effective exercises for reducing side fat of waist. Here's how to do them:

  1. Lie down on your back with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground.
  2. Place your hands behind your head and lift your head and shoulders off the ground.
  3. Twist your torso to the right and bring your right elbow towards your left knee.
  4. Lower your head and shoulders back to the ground.
  5. Repeat on the other side.

Do 3 sets of 15-20 reps on each side.

Side Plank Dips

Side plank dips are another great exercise for reducing side fat of waist. Here's how to do them:

  1. Start in a side plank position with your elbow on the ground and your feet stacked on top of each other.
  2. Dip your hips down towards the ground and then lift them back up.
  3. Repeat on the other side.

Do 3 sets of 15-20 reps on each side.

Woodchoppers

Woodchoppers are a full-body exercise that also targets the obliques. Here's how to do them:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a weight or medicine ball in both hands.
  2. Twist your torso to the right and raise the weight or medicine ball above your right shoulder.
  3. Lower the weight or medicine ball diagonally across your body towards your left knee.
  4. Twist your torso to the left and raise the weight or medicine ball above your left shoulder.
  5. Lower the weight or medicine ball diagonally across your body towards your right knee.

Do 3 sets of 15-20 reps on each side.

Bicycle Crunches

Bicycle crunches are a great exercise for toning the obliques and the abs. Here's how to do them:

  1. Lie down on your back with your knees bent and your hands behind your head.
  2. Bring your right elbow towards your left knee while straightening your right leg.
  3. Switch sides and bring your left elbow towards your right knee while straightening your left leg.

Do 3 sets of 15-20 reps on each side.

Leg Raises

Leg raises are a great exercise for the lower abs and the obliques. Here's how to do them:

  1. Lie down on your back with your legs straight and your hands by your sides.
  2. Lift your legs off the ground and towards the ceiling.
  3. Lower your legs back down towards the ground.

Do 3 sets of 15-20 reps.
