How Long Should I Do Fasted Cardio In The Morning

Are you looking to incorporate fasted cardio into your morning routine? Fasted cardio, or doing cardio exercise on an empty stomach, has become popular in recent years as a way to help burn fat. But how long should you do it for maximum benefits? In this article, we’ll go over the recommended duration of fasted cardio in the morning, as well as some tips to make the most of your workout.

Table of Contents:

  1. Recommended Duration
  2. Tips for Fasted Cardio
  3. FAQs
  4. Pros and Cons

Recommended Duration

The recommended duration for fasted cardio in the morning is generally 30-60 minutes. This is because when you exercise on an empty stomach, your body is forced to use stored fat as fuel instead of carbohydrates from food. Doing cardio for this duration helps to maximize fat burning and improve cardiovascular health.

However, it’s important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. If you’re new to fasted cardio, start with a shorter duration and gradually increase over time. You should also make sure to stay hydrated and nourished throughout the day to replenish your body after the workout.

Tips for Fasted Cardio

Here are some tips to make the most of your fasted cardio workout:

  • Drink plenty of water before and after your workout to stay hydrated.
  • Eat a healthy meal the night before to ensure your body has enough energy for the workout.
  • Start with a warm-up to prevent injury and prepare your body for the workout.
  • Choose low-impact exercises like walking, cycling or swimming to reduce the risk of injury.
  • Listen to your body and don’t push yourself too hard, especially if you’re new to fasted cardio.

FAQs

1. Can I drink coffee before fasted cardio?

Yes, you can drink coffee before fasted cardio. In fact, caffeine has been shown to help improve performance during workouts and increase fat burning. However, make sure to drink it black or with a small amount of unsweetened almond milk or cream to avoid breaking the fast.

2. Is fasted cardio better than regular cardio?

There is some evidence to suggest that fasted cardio may help increase fat burning and improve insulin sensitivity. However, regular cardio can also be effective for weight loss and cardiovascular health. It ultimately depends on your personal preference and goals.

3. Is fasted cardio safe?

Fasted cardio can be safe for most people, but it’s important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. If you have any health concerns or medical conditions, it’s best to consult with your doctor before starting a new exercise routine.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • May help increase fat burning
  • May improve insulin sensitivity
  • Can be an effective way to start the day

Cons:

  • Can be difficult to maintain for some people
  • May lead to muscle loss if done excessively
  • Can be risky for people with certain health conditions

In conclusion, fasted cardio can be a great addition to your morning routine for weight loss and cardiovascular health. However, it’s important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard. Start with a shorter duration and gradually increase over time, and make sure to stay hydrated and nourished throughout the day.
