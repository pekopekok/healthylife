What To Do On Bourbon Street During The Day A Relaxing Guide

Welcome to Bourbon Street, one of the most famous streets in New Orleans. Known for its vibrant nightlife, Bourbon Street is also a great place to explore during the day. In this guide, we’ll show you the best things to do on Bourbon Street during the day in a relaxed and stress-free way. From historic landmarks to delicious food, there’s something for everyone on Bourbon Street. Let’s get started!

History of Bourbon Street

Bourbon Street dates back to the 18th century, when it was named after the French royal family, the House of Bourbon. Since then, it has been a hub for business and entertainment in New Orleans. Today, Bourbon Street is lined with historic buildings, jazz clubs, and restaurants.

Shopping on Bourbon Street

Bourbon Street is home to a variety of shops, from souvenir stores to high-end boutiques. If you’re looking for unique gifts or New Orleans-themed items, check out Fleurty Girl or Cajun Clothing Co. For designer fashion and luxury goods, head to Royal Street, which intersects with Bourbon Street.

Eating on Bourbon Street

No trip to New Orleans is complete without trying the local cuisine. Bourbon Street has a range of dining options, from casual cafes to fine dining restaurants. For classic Southern dishes like gumbo and jambalaya, try Gumbo Shop or Court of Two Sisters. If you’re in the mood for seafood, Acme Oyster House is a local favorite.

Live Music on Bourbon Street

Bourbon Street is famous for its live music scene. During the day, you can catch performances by jazz bands, blues singers, and more. If you’re looking for a laid-back atmosphere, head to Fritzel’s European Jazz Pub. For a more lively experience, check out the Cat’s Meow or the Famous Door.

Sightseeing on Bourbon Street

Bourbon Street has several historic landmarks and attractions to explore. The most famous is the St. Louis Cathedral, a stunning church that dates back to the 18th century. You can also visit the Old Ursuline Convent, which now serves as a museum. For a unique experience, take a haunted history tour of Bourbon Street.

Relaxing on Bourbon Street

If you’re looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of Bourbon Street, there are several parks and green spaces nearby. The Woldenberg Riverfront Park offers scenic views of the Mississippi River, while Lafayette Square is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city.

Tips for Visiting Bourbon Street during the Day

  • Wear comfortable shoes, as the sidewalks can be uneven.
  • Stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close.
  • Plan your visit for mid-week, when the crowds are smaller.
  • Take breaks and relax in one of the nearby parks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bourbon Street safe during the day?

Yes, Bourbon Street is generally safe during the day. However, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close.

What is the best time to visit Bourbon Street?

The best time to visit Bourbon Street depends on your preferences. If you’re looking for a lively atmosphere, visit during the evening or on weekends. If you prefer a more relaxed experience, visit during the day or mid-week.

Can I bring my own drinks on Bourbon Street?

No, it is illegal to bring your own drinks on Bourbon Street. However, you can purchase drinks from the numerous bars and restaurants along the street.

What is the dress code for Bourbon Street?

There is no dress code for Bourbon Street, but it’s recommended to wear comfortable clothing and shoes.

Are there any family-friendly activities on Bourbon Street?

Yes, there are several family-friendly activities on Bourbon Street, including the Historic New Orleans Collection and the New Orleans Musical Legends Park.

Conclusion

Bourbon Street offers a wide range of activities and attractions during the day. From shopping and dining to live music and sightseeing, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. By following our tips and taking breaks throughout the day, you can relax and explore Bourbon Street stress-free.
