What is High Tea?

High tea, also known as afternoon tea, is a British tradition that involves a light meal served in the late afternoon or early evening. The meal typically includes tea, finger sandwiches, scones, and other sweet treats. High tea is a social event that is enjoyed with friends or family, and it is often served in a formal setting.

High Tea Definition Français

In French culture, high tea is known as "Le Thé de l'Après-midi," which translates to "The Afternoon Tea." The concept of high tea is the same in French culture as it is in British culture, with a few minor differences in the food and tea served.

How to Prepare High Tea

Preparing high tea is relatively simple, and it can be done at home or in a restaurant. Here are the steps to prepare high tea:

  1. Choose the tea: The first step in preparing high tea is to choose the tea you want to serve. Black tea is the most common tea served, but you can also choose green tea or herbal tea.
  2. Prepare the tea: Boil water and steep the tea for 3-5 minutes, depending on the type of tea you're using.
  3. Prepare the food: Prepare finger sandwiches, scones, and other treats to serve with the tea.
  4. Serve the tea: Serve the tea and food on a tiered stand or platter, and enjoy with friends or family.

Tips for Enjoying High Tea

Here are some tips for enjoying high tea:

  • Dress up: High tea is a formal event, so dress up and enjoy the occasion.
  • Try different teas: Experiment with different teas to find your favorite.
  • Take your time: High tea is meant to be enjoyed slowly, so take your time and savor the experience.

Pros

FAQs

What is the difference between high tea and afternoon tea?

High tea and afternoon tea are the same thing. In some countries, such as the United States, high tea is referred to as afternoon tea.

Can high tea be served in a casual setting?

Yes, high tea can be served in a casual setting, but it is traditionally served in a formal setting.

What is the proper etiquette for high tea?

The proper etiquette for high tea is to hold the teacup with your fingers and not your whole hand, and to stir the tea gently with a spoon. It is also polite to wait for everyone to be served before eating or drinking.
