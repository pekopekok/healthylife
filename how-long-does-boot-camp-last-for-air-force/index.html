Duration of Air Force Boot Camp

Air Force Boot Camp, officially known as Basic Military Training (BMT), is a 7.5 week training program held at Lackland Air Force Base in San Antonio, Texas. During this training period, recruits will undergo rigorous physical, mental, and emotional training to prepare them for their roles as airmen.

The first week of BMT is known as Zero Week, during which recruits will undergo in-processing, medical and dental examinations, and complete administrative tasks. The remaining 6.5 weeks are divided into four phases, each focusing on different aspects of military training:

  • Phase 1: Basic Expeditionary Airman Skills Training (BEAST), which focuses on field training and survival skills.
  • Phase 2: Air Force Core Values, which teaches recruits about the core values of the Air Force and how to apply them in their daily lives.
  • Phase 3: Basic War Skills, which focuses on combat training, including weapons handling, marksmanship, and chemical, biological, and nuclear defense.
  • Phase 4: Airmanship Training, which teaches recruits about Air Force history, customs and courtesies, and drill and ceremony.

Requirements to Join Air Force Boot Camp

Before enrolling in Air Force Boot Camp, there are several requirements that you must meet:

  • Be between 17 and 39 years old
  • Be a U.S. citizen or legal permanent resident
  • Have a high school diploma or equivalent
  • Meet minimum physical and medical requirements
  • Pass the Armed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery (ASVAB) test

If you meet these requirements and are interested in enrolling in Air Force Boot Camp, you can contact a recruiter to get started.

What to Expect During Air Force Boot Camp

Air Force Boot Camp is a challenging experience that will push you both physically and mentally. During the training period, you can expect to:

  • Wake up early and complete daily physical training (PT)
  • Attend classes and complete assignments on topics such as military customs and courtesies, self-aid and buddy care, and weapons handling
  • Participate in field training exercises and simulated combat scenarios
  • Live in dormitories with other recruits and follow strict rules and regulations
  • Be evaluated on your performance throughout the training period

Tips on How to Prepare for Air Force Boot Camp

To prepare for Air Force Boot Camp, it is important to:

  • Stay physically fit by exercising regularly and following a healthy diet
  • Practice good time management and organizational skills
  • Study and prepare for the ASVAB test
  • Research and familiarize yourself with Air Force core values and customs and courtesies
  • Talk to current or former airmen about their experiences in boot camp and ask for advice

FAQs About Air Force Boot Camp

How much do you get paid during Air Force Boot Camp?

Recruits are paid according to their rank and time in service. In 2023, the base pay for an Air Force recruit is $1,733.10 per month.

Can you bring your phone to Air Force Boot Camp?

No, recruits are not allowed to bring personal electronic devices, including cell phones, to Air Force Boot Camp. However, they will have access to pay phones and computers during designated times.

What happens if you fail Air Force Boot Camp?

If a recruit fails Air Force Boot Camp, they may be given the opportunity to restart the training program. However, if they fail again, they may be discharged from the Air Force.

Pros and Cons of Air Force Boot Camp

Pros:

  • Provides valuable training and skills that can be used both in the military and civilian life
  • Offers opportunities for personal growth and development
  • Can lead to a rewarding career in the Air Force

Cons:

  • Can be physically and mentally challenging
  • Requires a significant time commitment
  • May involve separation from family and friends
