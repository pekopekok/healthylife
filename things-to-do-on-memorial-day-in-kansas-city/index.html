Memorial Day is a day of remembrance and honor for the men and women who have died while serving in the United States Armed Forces. It is also a day to spend time with family and friends, and enjoy the start of the summer season. If you are in Kansas City on Memorial Day, there are plenty of things to do to celebrate this special day. In this article, we will highlight some of the best things to do on Memorial Day in Kansas City.

1. Visit the National World War I Museum and Memorial

The National World War I Museum and Memorial is a great place to visit on Memorial Day. This museum is dedicated to the history of World War I and the sacrifices made by the men and women who served in the war. The museum has a wide range of exhibits, including artifacts from the war, personal stories, and interactive exhibits. You can also visit the Liberty Memorial Tower, which offers a great view of the city.

If you are a history buff, this is a must-visit attraction. The museum is open from 10 am to 5 pm on Memorial Day, and admission is $18 for adults and $12 for children.

2. Attend the Kansas City Royals Game

If you are a sports fan, attending a Kansas City Royals game on Memorial Day is a great way to celebrate. The Royals usually have a game on Memorial Day, and they often have special promotions and events to honor the military.

The game is held at Kauffman Stadium, which is located in the Truman Sports Complex. You can purchase tickets online or at the box office. Tickets start at $20.

3. Take a Memorial Day Weekend Trip to Lake of the Ozarks

Take a trip to Lake of the Ozarks on Memorial Day weekend. The lake is located about 170 miles from Kansas City and offers a variety of outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, and hiking.

You can rent a cabin or stay at one of the many resorts in the area. There are also plenty of restaurants and shops to explore.

4. Enjoy a BBQ in Your Own Backyard

Memorial Day is the unofficial start of the summer season, and what better way to celebrate than with a backyard BBQ? Invite some friends and family over, fire up the grill, and enjoy some delicious food and good company.

You can also make it a potluck and have everyone bring their favorite dish. Don't forget the patriotic decorations and music!

5. Visit the Liberty Memorial

The Liberty Memorial is a beautiful and historic landmark in Kansas City. It was built to honor the men and women who served in World War I, and it offers a great view of the city skyline.

The memorial is open on Memorial Day from 10 am to 5 pm, and admission is $18 for adults and $12 for children.

6. Watch the Memorial Day Parade

The Memorial Day Parade is held in downtown Kansas City and is a great way to honor the military and celebrate the holiday. The parade usually includes marching bands, military units, and floats.

The parade starts at 10 am and ends at 12 pm. It is free to attend, and there are plenty of parking options in the area.

7. Attend a Memorial Day Concert

Many cities and towns host Memorial Day concerts to honor the military and celebrate the holiday. In Kansas City, the Kansas City Symphony usually holds a special Memorial Day concert at the Kauffman Center for the Performing Arts.

The concert usually starts at 8 pm, and ticket prices vary. Check the Kansas City Symphony website for more information.

8. Take a Stroll through the Overland Park Arboretum

The Overland Park Arboretum is a beautiful outdoor space that is perfect for a Memorial Day stroll. The arboretum features gardens, trails, and a variety of plant and animal life.

The arboretum is open on Memorial Day from 9 am to 5 pm, and admission is $3 for adults and $1 for children.

9. Explore the Kansas City Zoo

The Kansas City Zoo is a great place to spend Memorial Day with the family. The zoo features a variety of animals, including lions, tigers, and bears.

The zoo is open on Memorial Day from 9:30 am to 5 pm, and admission is $16.50 for adults and $13.50 for children.

10. Go Fishing at Smithville Lake

If you enjoy fishing, Smithville Lake is a great place to spend Memorial Day. The lake is located about 25 miles from Kansas City and offers a variety of fish species, including bass, crappie, and catfish.

You can rent a boat or fish from the shore. Don't forget to bring your fishing license!

Conclusion

Memorial Day is a special day to honor the men and women who have made the ultimate sacrifice for our country. It is also a day to spend time with family and friends and celebrate the start of the summer season. If you are in Kansas City on Memorial Day, there are plenty of things to do to celebrate this important day. From visiting museums to attending concerts to enjoying a backyard BBQ, there is something for everyone. We hope this article has given you some ideas for how to celebrate Memorial Day in Kansas City.
