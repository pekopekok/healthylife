If you are new to weight training or looking for a new workout routine, you may have heard of the push pull legs (PPL) workout. But is push pull legs for beginners? In this article, we will explore what the PPL workout is, its benefits and drawbacks, and whether it is suitable for beginners.

What is Push Pull Legs (PPL) Workout?

The push pull legs workout is a weight training routine that divides exercises into three categories: push, pull, and legs. The push exercises work the muscles involved in pushing, such as chest, triceps, and shoulders. The pull exercises work the muscles involved in pulling, such as back, biceps, and forearms. The leg exercises work the muscles in the lower body, such as quads, hamstrings, and glutes.

How to do the Push Pull Legs (PPL) Workout?

The PPL workout is typically done over a three-day period, with each day focusing on one of the three categories of exercises. For example, Monday is push day, Wednesday is pull day, and Friday is leg day. The workout can be customized to fit individual needs and preferences, but a sample workout routine may include the following:

Push Day:

  • Barbell bench press
  • Incline dumbbell press
  • Seated military press
  • Triceps pushdown

Pull Day:

  • Barbell rows
  • Lat pulldowns
  • Seated cable rows
  • Bicep curls

Leg Day:

  • Squats
  • Deadlifts
  • Leg press
  • Calf raises

Tips for Beginners

If you are new to weight training or the PPL workout, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with light weights and focus on proper form
  • Gradually increase the weight as you get stronger
  • Include rest days in your workout routine to allow for muscle recovery
  • Consult with a personal trainer or healthcare professional if you have any concerns or medical conditions

Pros and Cons of PPL Workout

Like any workout routine, the PPL workout has its advantages and disadvantages:

Pros:

  • Targets all major muscle groups
  • Allows for customization and variation
  • Can be done with minimal equipment
  • Can be effective for muscle growth and strength

Cons:

  • May not be suitable for beginners or those with medical conditions
  • Requires proper form and technique to avoid injury
  • May not be as effective for advanced lifters or those with specific fitness goals
  • Can be time-consuming for those with busy schedules

FAQs

Is the PPL workout suitable for beginners?

The PPL workout can be suitable for beginners, but it is important to start with light weights and focus on proper form. It is also important to consult with a personal trainer or healthcare professional if you have any concerns or medical conditions.

How often should I do the PPL workout?

The PPL workout is typically done over a three-day period, with each day focusing on one of the three categories of exercises. Rest days should be included in the workout routine to allow for muscle recovery.

What equipment do I need for the PPL workout?

The PPL workout can be done with minimal equipment, such as barbells, dumbbells, and cables. However, access to a gym or fitness center may be necessary for some exercises.

Solution

If you are looking for a weight training routine that targets all major muscle groups and allows for customization and variation, the PPL workout may be worth considering. However, it is important to start with light weights and focus on proper form, and consult with a personal trainer or healthcare professional if you have any concerns or medical conditions. With dedication and consistency, the PPL workout can be effective for muscle growth and strength.

Conclusion

The push pull legs workout can be a great option for beginners and experienced lifters alike. It targets all major muscle groups and allows for customization and variation. However, it is important to start with light weights and focus on proper form, and consult with a personal trainer or healthcare professional if you have any concerns or medical conditions. With dedication and consistency, the PPL workout can be effective for muscle growth and strength.
