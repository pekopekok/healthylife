What does "go out" mean?

The phrase "go out" is a phrasal verb that has multiple meanings depending on the context and situation. At its most basic level, it means to leave one's home or current location to go to another place. However, it can also mean to date or be in a romantic relationship with someone, to stop burning, to be extinguished or to stop functioning, and to become available or be released for public consumption.

Contexts and situations where "go out" is used

The phrase "go out" is a versatile phrase that can be used in various contexts and situations. Here are some common uses:

  • Going out for a walk, run, or jog
  • Going out to eat at a restaurant
  • Going out to a party, bar, or club
  • Going out of town or on vacation
  • Going out on a date or being in a romantic relationship
  • Turning off a light or fire
  • Stopping the functioning of a machine or electronic device
  • Releasing a product or item for public consumption

Sentence examples using "go out"

Here are some sentence examples using "go out" in different contexts:

  • I'm going out for a run in the morning.
  • Do you want to go out to eat tonight?
  • We're going out to celebrate our friend's birthday.
  • My family and I are going out of town for the weekend.
  • Are you guys going out or staying in tonight?
  • The fire in the fireplace finally went out after several hours.
  • The printer stopped working and then went out completely.
  • The new iPhone is going to go out on the market next month.

Tips for using "go out" in conversations

The phrase "go out" usually means leaving a current location to go to another place, while "go outside" means stepping out of a building or enclosed space.
