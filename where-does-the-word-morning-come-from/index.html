Etymology of Morning

The word morning comes from the Old English word "morgen," which means "dawn" or "early part of the day." This word is also related to the German word "morgen" and the Dutch word "morgen," both of which mean "morning."

Interestingly, the word morgen is also related to the word "tomorrow." In Old English, "to morgen" meant "to the morning," which eventually evolved to mean "tomorrow."

Evolution of the Word Morning

Over time, the word morning has evolved and changed in both pronunciation and meaning. In Middle English, the word was pronounced "morn," and it was used to refer to the period between dawn and noon.

By the 16th century, the word had evolved to its current pronunciation and was used to refer to the early part of the day, from dawn until noon. Today, morning is generally considered to be the time between sunrise and noon.

Usage of Morning in Modern English

In modern English, morning is used in a variety of ways. It can be used as a greeting, as in "Good morning!" It can also be used to refer to the time of day, as in "I like to exercise in the morning."

The word morning is also commonly used in idiomatic expressions, such as "the early bird catches the worm" or "I'm not a morning person." In these expressions, morning is used to refer to the early part of the day and the habits or characteristics of people during that time.
